# Lunar Eclipse - Basic Explanation

A lunar eclipse occurs when Earth passes between the Sun and the Moon, causing Earth's shadow to fall on the Moon. Unlike solar eclipses, lunar eclipses are safe to view with the naked eye.

## Types of Lunar Eclipses

**Total Lunar Eclipse**: The Moon passes completely through Earth's umbra (full shadow). The Moon often appears reddish due to sunlight refracted through Earth's atmosphere, creating the "blood moon" effect.

**Partial Lunar Eclipse**: Only part of the Moon enters Earth's umbra.

**Penumbral Lunar Eclipse**: The Moon passes through Earth's penumbra (partial shadow), causing subtle darkening that is often difficult to notice.

## Frequency and Visibility

Lunar eclipses are visible from anywhere on Earth's night side, making them observable by roughly half the planet. They occur 2-4 times per year on average.

## Duration

Total lunar eclipses can last over an hour, much longer than total solar eclipses which last only a few minutes at most.
