# Solar Eclipse - Basic Explanation

A solar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes between Earth and the Sun, blocking all or part of the Sun's light from reaching Earth. This alignment creates a shadow on Earth's surface.

## Types of Solar Eclipses

**Total Solar Eclipse**: The Moon completely covers the Sun's disk. Observers in the path of totality experience darkness during daytime.

**Partial Solar Eclipse**: The Moon covers only part of the Sun. This is visible from a larger geographic area.

**Annular Solar Eclipse**: The Moon is farther from Earth and appears smaller, creating a "ring of fire" effect where the Sun's edges remain visible.

## Why Eclipses Don't Happen Every Month

The Moon's orbit is tilted about 5 degrees relative to Earth's orbit around the Sun. Most months, the Moon passes above or below the Sun from Earth's perspective. Eclipses occur only when the alignment happens near the orbital crossing points called nodes.

## Safety Note

Never look directly at the Sun during an eclipse without proper solar filters. Regular sunglasses are not safe for eclipse viewing.
