# Moon Phases - Scientific Explanation

Moon phases result from the changing angle between the Sun, Moon, and Earth as the Moon orbits our planet. The Moon doesn't produce its own light; we see it because it reflects sunlight.

## The Eight Primary Phases

1. **New Moon**: The Moon is between Earth and Sun. The illuminated side faces away from us, making the Moon nearly invisible.

2. **Waxing Crescent**: A thin sliver of the Moon becomes visible as it moves in its orbit.

3. **First Quarter**: Half of the Moon's visible face is illuminated. Called "first quarter" because the Moon has completed one-quarter of its orbit.

4. **Waxing Gibbous**: More than half but not all of the Moon is visible.

5. **Full Moon**: The Moon is opposite the Sun, and its entire visible face is illuminated.

6. **Waning Gibbous**: The illuminated portion begins to decrease.

7. **Last Quarter**: Half of the Moon is visible again, but the opposite half from first quarter.

8. **Waning Crescent**: Only a thin sliver remains visible before returning to new moon.

## Orbital Period

The Moon completes one orbit around Earth approximately every 29.5 days, which is why we see the full cycle of phases roughly once per month.

## Why We See Phases

The key concept: the Moon's phase depends on its position relative to the Sun and Earth. The Moon is always half-illuminated by the Sun, but we see different portions of that illuminated half depending on the Moon's orbital position.
