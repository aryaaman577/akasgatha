# Earth Rotation and Day-Night Cycle

Day and night result from Earth's rotation on its axis. Earth spins once approximately every 24 hours, causing different parts of the planet to face toward and away from the Sun.

## Rotation Mechanics

Earth rotates from west to east, which is why the Sun appears to rise in the east and set in the west. This rotation is continuous and nearly constant in speed.

## Why 24 Hours

One complete rotation relative to the Sun (a solar day) takes about 24 hours. Earth actually completes one rotation relative to distant stars in about 23 hours and 56 minutes (a sidereal day), but the difference accounts for Earth's orbital motion around the Sun.

## Twilight Zones

Sunrise and sunset aren't instantaneous because Earth's atmosphere scatters sunlight, creating twilight periods when the Sun is below the horizon but sky remains partially lit.

## No "Dark Side"

Earth doesn't have a permanently dark side. Every part of Earth (except the extreme polar regions) experiences day and night as the planet rotates.

## Rotation Speed

At the equator, Earth's surface moves at about 1,670 kilometers per hour due to rotation. We don't feel this motion because everything on Earth—including the atmosphere—moves together.
