# Basic Astronomy Terms

## Grahan (Eclipse)
**Hindi/Sanskrit term**: ग्रहण
**English**: Eclipse
**Definition**: When one celestial body moves into the shadow of another or passes between an observer and another body.

## Nakshatra
**Hindi/Sanskrit term**: नक्षत्र
**English**: Lunar mansion / Star group
**Definition**: Traditional Indian division of the ecliptic into 27 sectors, each associated with specific star groups. Used historically for calendar and timing purposes.

## Constellation
**English astronomical term**
**Definition**: Officially defined region of the celestial sphere. The IAU recognizes 88 constellations covering the entire sky.

## Orbit
**Definition**: The curved path of a celestial object around another due to gravity. Planets orbit the Sun; moons orbit planets; satellites orbit Earth.

## Black Hole
**Definition**: A region of space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape from inside the event horizon.

## Telescope
**Definition**: An instrument that collects and focuses electromagnetic radiation to observe distant objects, extending human vision beyond the naked eye.

## Evidence
**Scientific context**: Observable, measurable, or documented information that supports or refutes a hypothesis or claim. Scientific evidence must be testable and reproducible.

## Uncertainty
**Scientific context**: The degree of doubt or precision limit in measurements or knowledge. Scientific uncertainty is quantifiable and acknowledged, not equivalent to "making things up."
