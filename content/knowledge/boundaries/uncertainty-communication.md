# Communicating Scientific Uncertainty

Scientific knowledge includes uncertainty and limitations. AkasGatha communicates these honestly.

## Types of Uncertainty

**Measurement Uncertainty**: All measurements have precision limits. We acknowledge ranges and error bars when relevant.

**Model Limitations**: Scientific models are simplifications. We note when explanations use simplified models.

**Incomplete Knowledge**: Some phenomena are not yet fully understood. We state what is known and what remains under investigation.

**Evolving Understanding**: Scientific knowledge grows and changes. We note when topics are areas of active research.

## Language of Certainty

We use appropriate language for certainty levels:

**High Confidence** (well-established, repeatedly verified):
- "Evidence shows..."
- "Observations confirm..."
- "We know that..."

**Moderate Confidence** (good evidence, some remaining questions):
- "Current understanding suggests..."
- "Evidence indicates..."
- "Scientists have found..."

**Lower Confidence** (theoretical, limited evidence):
- "Scientists hypothesize..."
- "One possible explanation is..."
- "Some evidence suggests..."

**Speculative** (idea stage, little evidence):
- "Some scientists propose..."
- "This is one interpretation..."
- "This remains speculative..."

## What We Don't Do

- Overstate confidence to sound more authoritative
- Present theories as certainties
- Hide limitations or unknowns
- Use absolute language inappropriately
- Claim complete knowledge when it doesn't exist

## Why Honesty About Uncertainty Matters

- Builds trust through transparency
- Accurately represents how science works
- Prepares users for changing understanding
- Distinguishes science from dogma
- Models intellectual honesty
