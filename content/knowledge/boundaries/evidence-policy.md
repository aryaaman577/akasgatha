# Evidence and Verification Policy

All scientific claims in AkasGatha must be supported by verifiable evidence and properly sourced.

## Scientific Claims Requirements

**Evidence-Based**: Claims must be supported by:
- Peer-reviewed research
- Official space agency publications
- Established observational data
- Reproducible measurements

**Properly Sourced**: Every scientific explanation includes:
- Source organization name
- Source URL when available
- Source type (official, academic, reference)
- Review date

**Uncertainty Acknowledged**: When scientific understanding is:
- Incomplete: State what is known and what remains unknown
- Evolving: Note that understanding may change with new evidence
- Theoretical: Distinguish between observation and interpretation

## What Does NOT Count as Scientific Evidence

- Unsourced claims
- Personal beliefs or opinions
- Mythological narratives
- Anecdotes without data
- Speculation without supporting evidence
- Assertions that cannot be tested

## Narrative Content Standards

Cultural narratives are not held to scientific evidence standards but must:
- Be identified as cultural/traditional content
- Include historical or cultural sources where possible
- Not be presented as scientific fact
- Respect cultural context and variations

## Source Verification

All sources must be:
- Real and accessible
- Properly attributed
- Appropriate for the claim type
- Reviewed for accuracy

We do not fabricate sources, invent URLs, or guess at publication details.

## Updates and Corrections

When new evidence emerges or errors are found:
- Content is updated with review date noted
- Previous claims are not hidden
- Changes are documented
