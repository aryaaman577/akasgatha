# Katha and Vigyan Separation Policy

AkasGatha maintains strict separation between cultural narratives (Katha) and scientific knowledge (Vigyan) to preserve the integrity of both domains.

## Core Principle

**Katha (Narrative)** and **Vigyan (Science)** answer different types of questions and use different methods. Neither domain should be misrepresented as the other.

## Katha Domain

Cultural narratives including:
- Traditional stories about celestial phenomena
- Symbolic interpretations
- Mythological frameworks
- Philosophical and ethical teachings encoded in sky-stories
- Historical cultural practices related to astronomy

**Purpose**: Cultural meaning, values transmission, memorable frameworks

**Method**: Storytelling, symbolism, tradition

**Verification**: Historical and cultural scholarship, textual analysis

## Vigyan Domain

Scientific knowledge including:
- Observable, measurable phenomena
- Testable hypotheses and theories
- Reproducible observations
- Evidence-based explanations
- Physical mechanisms and laws

**Purpose**: Understanding physical mechanisms, prediction, explanation

**Method**: Observation, measurement, testing, mathematical modeling

**Verification**: Empirical evidence, peer review, reproducibility

## Why Separation Matters

1. **Preserves scientific integrity**: Science remains testable and evidence-based
2. **Respects cultural heritage**: Narratives are valued for their cultural meaning, not forced into scientific frameworks
3. **Prevents confusion**: Users understand what type of knowledge they're receiving
4. **Maintains honesty**: No false claims about ancient texts predicting modern discoveries
5. **Educational clarity**: Different types of questions require different types of answers

## Implementation

- Clear labeling of all content by domain
- No mixing of claims within single explanations
- Explicit identification of sources and their types
- Recognition of uncertainty and evidence limits
- No mythology presented as physics
- No physics oversimplified into mythology
