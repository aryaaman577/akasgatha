# Citation and Source Policy

All knowledge in AkasGatha is properly attributed to verifiable sources.

## Source Requirements

Every piece of content must identify:
1. **Source Name**: Organization, publication, or text
2. **Source URL**: Valid link when available
3. **Source Type**: Official, academic, reference, internal-policy
4. **Review Date**: When content was last verified

## Source Type Definitions

**Official**: Government space agencies, established astronomical institutions
- Examples: NASA, ESA, ISRO, IAU

**Academic**: Peer-reviewed research, university publications, scientific journals
- Must be from recognized academic institutions

**Reference**: Encyclopedic references, established educational resources
- Must be authoritative and maintained

**Primary-Text**: Historical texts, archived manuscripts
- Must include provenance and translation notes

**Internal-Policy**: AkasGatha project policies and guidelines
- Clearly marked as internal

## Citation Identity

Every content chunk receives a stable citation identifier that connects retrieved information to its source document. Users can trace any piece of information back to its origin.

## What We Don't Do

- Fabricate sources or URLs
- Invent publication details
- Use unreliable sources without disclosure
- Present uncited information as authoritative
- Hide source limitations
- Create fake citation records

## Source Hierarchy

When multiple sources are available, prefer:
1. Primary authoritative sources (NASA, ESA, etc.)
2. Peer-reviewed academic publications
3. Educational resources from recognized institutions
4. General encyclopedic references

## Cultural and Historical Sources

For narrative content:
- Use recognized cultural and historical references
- Acknowledge when interpretations vary
- Note geographical and temporal context
- Respect cultural complexity

## Verification Process

Sources must be:
- Accessible and verifiable
- Appropriate for the claim
- Actually containing the information cited
- Current or historically significant
